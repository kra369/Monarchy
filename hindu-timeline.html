1772 - Anglo-Hindu Law 1772  (first implemented in Bengal) fashioned on Christian law that replaced traditional law Hindu inheritance laws were always equitable

Hindu inheritance laws were always equitable. It was the 1761 Succession Act fashioned on Christian law that replaced traditional law, and dispossessed women. Incidentally, the practice of sati saw a peak thereafter (therefore also in Bengal, since that's where it was first

enacted) because the new law made widowed women undesirable claimants to property of marital family, being divested of right over dowry and matrilineal property, rendered as liabilities. All social aberrations: dowry as it was subsequently practiced (demanded and claimed

by in-laws, instead of being rightful bridal property), sati, shunting destitute widows to pilgrimages, etc. directly follow from this.
